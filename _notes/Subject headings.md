# What are subject headings?

You can think of these as tags that are used to catalog the articles in a particular database. These are different than [keywords](Keywords.md), which they should be combined _with_.

The language for subject headings comes from controlled vocabularies, which are like a standardized thesaurus that allows us to search for themes in a consistent manner.

## MeSH Headings

The image below is a screenshot from the National Library of Medicine's MeSH Headings database. These are the subject headings you'll use when conducting a search in PubMed, for example. 

![](https://i.imgur.com/wTgkXFc.png)

It shows us that one of the subject headings in its database is "**Drug Users**". There is a short definition of the term provided, as well as a list of "entry terms". These are terms that will be captured in your search when you use the MeSH heading they are listed under.

## Example: MeSH Headings in [PubMed](PubMed.md)

The National Library of Medicine has a website where anyone can search their database of subject headings. If we were to search one of our main concepts for the review, "people who use drugs", the results would be the list of terms below.

![](https://i.imgur.com/XrtygxK.png)

This means that there are papers in PubMed's database which are indexed using this specific language. So even though we might cringe at the inclusion of terms like "Drug Addict", we might want to use it in our search. The closest thing we get to the actual term we were looking for is "people who _inject_ drugs". This is why, although helpful in making sure we cast a wide net, we are still going to come up with our own language for the search. That's when we'll start thinking about which [keywords](Keywords.md) we want to use.
